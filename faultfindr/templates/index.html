About

We live in a world that is becoming ever more reliant on technology and our interactions with computers have become central to our lives. Our computers are not just tools that we use, but extensions of ourselves. A computer failure can be devastating due to loss of data that was not backed up, time spent without a computer, frustrating interactions with customer service, and potentially a time consuming search for a new device.

FaultFindr is a platform that leverages Amazon product reviews to identify failure modes and estimate failure rates for laptops so that those who are in the market for a new laptop can minimize the likelihood of experiencing such product failure. Since laptops are complicated devices, there are many ways in which they can fail. We try to elucidate the failure modes by extracting excerpts from reviews that describe a particular failure, and to give a distribution of the laptop components that are being discussed within reviews that describe a particular failure.

In order to identify those reviews that discuss product failure, we perform some natural language processing on the review text, and feed each sentence in each review into a series of trained Naive Bayes models that make predictions on whether or not individual sentences discuss product failure, and which laptop components the reviews are also discussing.

Currently FaultFindr is built on a historical database of about 15000 laptop reviews that span from 2000-2014 since Amazon does not provide product reviews in its API and makes it difficult to scrape reviews from their site. Nevertheless, with access to the bulk of Amazon reviews, more recent products could be added to the database. This application was built as an Insight Data Science project. For more information, please see the slides below, see the code on my github page, or contact me.
